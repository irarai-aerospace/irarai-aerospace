Avionics and Recovery of 10k High Powered Reusable Rocket

Launching a rocket being an aerospace engineer seemed a must! Since there were not many rocket/spacecraft/avionics projects in my coursework, I set out to build my own! I joined the first ever sounding rocket in IIT Madras as an avionics and recovery engineer, our team was named Team Abhyuday which means to rise!(True Indeed). Sounding rockets are sub-orbital vehicles designed for scientific experiments, their reusability is a key feature for sustainable space exploration. I started off my work by understanding the avionics and recovery system.Eventually developed the avionics bay (Rocket's brain) for Chetak 1.0 (First Sounding Rocket) and established the circuit design for triggering launch of two parachutes at apogee and 1000ft above the ground.

24th June, 2023 marked history when we launched and recovered our rocket in it's very first attempt without any prior test Launch, but this wasn't enough I envisioned to develop in-house flight computers and optimized recovery systems. This came into application after I was appointed as the Head for Avionics and Recovery Subsystem. The point when we have to make pivotal decisions with people relying on you, really embedds the power of the quote : "Failure is not an option".

17th June, 2024 chetak 2.0 soared up into the sky etching IIT Madras as the best rocketry team in Asia!. Have never related to the screams of Joy after a successful launch better.

Commercial Delivery UAV - Conceptual Design
Advisors: Prof. D Muniraj and S. Ghosh

This was an interesting course project, where we had the freedom to choose our own mission statement and UAV design. I with a team of four more, addressed the challenges that come with the surge in delivery demands, this has led to traffic congestion and ultimately road accidents. Lack of scheduled delivery and safety of the deliverers was the main issue we wanted to focus. Therefore, we aimed to design a dual propulsion electric fixed wing VTOL for commercial delivery of goods.

The design is an Electric fixed wing configuration with quadplane configuration providing VTOL capabilities.

Max. Takeoff Weight - 23.54 kg
Payload Capacity - 4.4 kg
Range - 250 km
Cruise speed - 25m/s

Hybrid DCGAN Architecture using Ferroelectric Material for Stochasticity
Advisors: Prof. U Ganguly

Since half of my summer went in preparation of Launch of Chetak 1.0 in 2023, I decided to spend other half to explore another expect of my curiosity, fueled by Machine Learning courses in Stanford, "How to mimick the human Brain efficiently?"

MeLoDe Lab were the ideal place, where I had heard of reknowned Prof Udayan Ganguly working over Spiking Neural Networks and implementing neural networks to efficient hardware devices. I just had a month but it was enough to understand the challenges and possible research areas in the field.

I had many discussions with Dr Paritosh Mehar (Research Scholar) where I learned more then from just reading research articles. Serial Manipulator (Robotic Arm) Coppeliasim Simulation
Advisors: Prof. B Sebastian

I have been fascinated with the idea of Artificial General Intelligence and Humanoid Robotics. My fascination arose from the animation "Doremon", being a single child, it was fun to imagine a robot as humanoid as him as a sibling/company :D

This Project was part of my course in Introduction to Robotics, where we implemented the forward, inverse and differential kinematics to a robotic arm manipulator in Coppeliasim software.

Forward Kinematics: Process of calculating the end effector's (the tip of a robotic arm or manipulator) position and orientation in space based on the known joint parameters

Inverse Kinematics: Reverse process of forward kinematics. It involves calculating the joint parameters (angles or displacements) needed to reach a desired end-effector position and orientation.

Differential Kinematics: It focuses on the relationship between joint velocities and the velocity of the end effector. It involves calculating how small changes in joint positions (or velocities) affect the position and orientation of the end effector.
