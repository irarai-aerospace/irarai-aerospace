Identifying Higher order interactions in complex sytems
Advisor: Prof. R.I Sujith
Place: Centre of Excellence for Critical Transition in Complex Systems (CoE-CTCS) Lab, IIT, Madras

Nature is very complex due to various interactions, our means of understanding it most of the time is based on the idea of reductionism. I was amazed when I understood how we have a whole domain to take account of various interactions leading to better understanding of such systems! In our lab we work on studying critical transitions in complex system, such critical transitions can have undesirable or even catastrophic consequences including failure of space missions, power blackouts, extreme weather, extinction of species in ecosystems and sudden crash of financial markets.

I started my work with the motivation to detect higher order interactions in the systems. Along the journey of developing the methos I studied different mathematical models like Ordinal Partition Networks, recurrence analysis, phase portraits, hypergraphs, simplicial complexes etc to understand these complex systems.

Currently I have developed a method to identify such interactions given multivariate time series which I am testing on different datasets.

Ordinal Partition Networks Ordinal Partition Networks (OPNs) are a method used in time series analysis to study the dynamics and structure of complex systems. The OPN method focuses on creating symbolic representations of time series data to uncover hidden patterns and connections.

Recurrence Plots Recurrence plots offer a visual and quantitative approach to identifying patterns and behaviors in complex time series data. By examining how often a system revisits similar states, they can reveal insights into stability, periodicity, and chaotic behavior.

In-Flight Validation via Thermal/Infrared Remote Sensing
Advisor: Dr Simon Hook
Place: NASA, Jet Propulsion Laboratory, California, US

I became the youngest undergraduate from India to earn my internship at NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory in 2024. Thermal Infrared (TIR) data are used in a wide variety of studies from monitoring urban heating to determining the mineral composition of the surface. Utilizing TIR data for such studies requires that the data are calibrated and validated. Calibration typically involves comparing the measurements to a blackbody in-flight and validation involves comparing the calibrated data to in situ measurements. NASA/JPL has established several long-term TIR validation sites that make all the measurements necessary to validate airborne and satellite thermal infrared data.

In particular to validate TIR data from recent campaigns with the MASTER and HyTES airborne sensors and ECOSTRESS and VIIRS satellite-sensor system.I used these data to create simulation datasets for use in algorithm development for future TIR satellite-sensor systems. My work showed the minimum number of months required for validating a satellite data , along with also creating Monte carlo simulation datasets.

Gravitational Lensing Stereoscopy
Advisor: Prof. Richard Gordan
Place: Online , University of Manitoba, Canada

PHASE-1

Can we view a galaxy in 3D? This was the motivation to carry out a research study to define the limits of viewing distant galaxies in 3D. The idea was inspired by 'triplet' image by Kelly taken by James Webb Space Telescope(JWST), where a single galaxy can be seen through multiple angle perspective due to gravitational lesning by a galactic cluster. I performed disparity checks and developed stereo-pairs from these galaxies.This was the first ever attempt to check the possibility of this endeavour, the results for which were published in arXiv in 2024.

PHASE-2

We continued the work by working on another manuscript which by using all possible stereoscopic techniques to give a simulation review of the current best technique to study galactic images in 3D. The summary papaer was accepted in 6th URSI Conference , where I presented my poster based on my 2 years of work, in process of publishing in IEEE.

Advisor: Prof. Vandana Vinayak
Place: Diatom Nano -Engineering Lab

PHASE-1

I often used to accompany my mother in high school in her research lab, which later sparked my interest in steering the focus towards space explorations. Space missions are expanding their horizons towards green energy. At the same time humans are expanding their invasion beyond the earth’s orbit and envisage to have a habitat on other planets like Mars. Hoping for interplanetary travel and inspired by International Space Station (ISS) missions ,I was interested in exploring the depths of this venture and so proceeded to do a literature survey to be published in Elsevier in 2022.

PHASE-2

I continued to further study the psychrophilic nature of diatoms and possibility of them to terraform Mars. The study for this was exciting and new!,Reducing payloads in space missions utilizing biowaste generated in space missions for self-sustainability survival of microalgae in photovoltaic fuel cells, resulting in high-value food products during space voyages and the possibility of terraforming Mars to a planet like ours. The results were published in Scrivener and WILEY in 2024 in Chapter 12 of Book "Diatom Cultivation for Biofuel, Food and High-Value Products".
