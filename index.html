Ira RAI

I am a first year MS student at Purdue University in the department of Aeronautics and Astronautics Engineering. At present I am focusing research in Prof. Kenshiro Oguri's Lab. My research is focused on controls, convex optimization applied to astrodynamics.

I did my undergraduate at the Department of Aerospace Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Madras.

I am also working with Prof. R.I. Sujith at IIT Madras, on developing methods for quantifying emergent higher order interaction in oscillator networks using data.

During summer 2024, I worked as an intern at the NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory. I worked on evaluating minimum months required for a satellite validation. In addition I prepared initial steps for studying the atmospheric profiles via Aqua MODIS data by evaluating statistical inferences and generating Monte Carlo Simulations.

In summer 2023, I worked in MeLoDe (Memory Logic and Devices) Lab in Indian Institute of Technology (IIT), Bombay, with whom I worked to simulate DCGANs and build a hybrid DCGAN architecture which uses ferroelectric material for stochasticity.

Since 2022, I am also working with Prof Richard Gordan, where we worked on a novel idea of "Gravitational Lensing Stereoscopy".


NOTE: I am applying for Internships in 2026.
